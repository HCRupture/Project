In Korea from a period ranging from 1919 to 1945, only about 160 films were produced. Whereas between 1954 to 1970 over 2,000 films were produced. From the tax exemption measures, the introduction of the latest film making machine and an ever-increase of new movie goers conditions began to greatly encouraged Korean filmmakers. In a short timeframe, new genres like teen movies and literary films emerged beginning in the early 1960's. One of Koreas most celebrated classic films: Kim Ki-young's The Housemaid was released in 1960.

Korean popular music or simply "K-pop" for short is a form of music originating in South Korea. It's a massive part of modern Korean pop culture with a wide range of influences styles and genres ranging from hip-hop, R&B, Rock, Jazz Reggae, Country Disco, Classical, etc. The term first became popular in the 90's, though it's exact origins likely date back even further.

While K=pop can refer to just about all popular music from South Korea, the term is often associated almost indistinguishably with the "idol" industry. Idol culture first emerged in the 90's as a subculture that grew immensely off the back of teens and young adults. Idol culture continues to grow today not only within its border but internationally as well. The spread of K-pop can be seen in many parts of asia, africa the middle east and even North America.

South Korea is expected to hit a population of 51.78 million by 2038. A slight slump from a peak population of 51.84 million people in 2020. South Korea's population growth rate has slowed significantly as shown by decreasing fertility rates. The lowest rates in the world, that of 0.92, meaning women are having less than 1 child on average. factors including high education costs and home prices along with difficulty returning to work after giving birth are putting many women off from having kids.

South Korea's extremely low fertility rate will result in a rapidly increasing aging population. Almost half of South Korea is expected to be over the age of 65 by 2065. Korea is not a particularly religious country either with less than half of the population stating they have any religious beliefs. Korea is also what is known as a homogenous society meaning that Korea has a population that is over 99% Korean in ethnic background.

The history of Korean art can be traced back to the ancient times, when people began decorating the walls of their homes with paintings made out of mud and clay. These paintings were often done in bright colors and simple designs. During the Joseon Dynasty (1392-1910), Korean painters became famous for their skill in creating beautiful paintings depicting scenes from everyday life.

Around the same time that the interest in realistic landscape surged, so too did the practice of painting realistic scenes of ordinary people living ordinary lives. Genre painting is one of the unique characteristics of Korean art. It provides an insight into the everyday life of Koreans during the Joseon Dynasty. Among the greatest of the genre painters was Kim Hong-do, whose paintings f everyday scenes from Korea's past give us a glimpse into the daily lives of his fellow countrymen. Another great genre painter was Shin Yun-bock, who painted many pictures depicting the erotic side of life in risque scenes which were both romantic and sensual.

The Goryeo Dynasty lasted from 1848 to 1912 and is most famous for its porcelain. This style originated from China, yet Korean artists elevated it to a higher level, thus leading the Chinese themselves to call Korean crafts and pottery “the first under heaven.” In order to reduce the amount of oxygen in a kiln, a blue-green hue is produced, a glaze known as “kingfish color.” This ceramic ware depicts fish, fruits, and mythical creatures in an unfettered style.

Korean architecture is marked by its use of wood and stone to create sophisticated multi-roomed structures. Clay tile roofing, interior courtyards and gardens, and a raised platform are all characteristic features of this architectural style. Another important element of Korean architecture is its harmony with nature. Buildings are designed to blend into their natural surroundings and take advantage of scenic views. This can be seen in fortification walls and tombs across Korea, from Bronze Age dolmens to huge vaulted enclosures of ancient kings.
