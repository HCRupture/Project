Ancient Korea

Prehistoric Korea

The earliest known settlements on the Korean peninsula date back to c. 6,000 BCE though it was inhabited as far back as 10,000 BCE by peoples who subsisted on hunting, fishing, and gathering. Roughly 200,000 primitive Megalithic structures, the oldest known structures to date, still remain on much of the landscape of Korea.

Three Kingdoms Period

The Three kingdoms of korea or the "Sahman" were three kingdoms (Goguryeo, Baekje and Silla) from which the modern name "Korea" was derived. The Period is defined today from 57BC to 668AD and was founded soon after the fall of "Gojoseon" a powerful federation that had ruled over the northern korean Peninsula as far back as 2300 BC. The Three kingdoms govermental system was much of what it would remain for the entirety of ancient Korea. Within it's system, a monarch ruled on top surrounded with aid of dministrative officials whom where of aristocracy. The majority of the kingdom population were divided into peasantry and slaves. Slaves were the bottom of the social hierarchy and consisted of people whose origins were prisoners of war or criminals. Slaves usually worked for and on the estates of aristocracy.

Modern Histroy

Korean Empire

The Korean Empire was proclaimed in 1897 lasting until 1910. The coronation of King Gojong as Emperor Gwangmu in 1897 was followed by dramatic reform for a series of events designed to raise the profile of the Korean Empire among the international community. During this brief period, Korea had some success in modernising many of its various industries including education, economy and its military. Many outside countries where all invested in Koreas future success and sought to influence it politically.

Annexation, War and Peace

In 1910, the militarily occupied Korea was forced into the Japan-Korea Annexation Treaty. Signed by Lee Wan-Yong, a General Power of Attorney by the Emperor. However, many believe the Emperor is said to have not actually ratified the treaty. There is a long dispute whether this treaty was legal or illegal due to its signing under duress, threat of force and bribes. Following Japan's surrender of WW2 in 1945, the United Nations developed plans for a trusteeship administration. The Soviet Union were administered the northern peninsula of the 38th parallel with the south occupied by The United States. The politics of which, reulted in the 1948 establishment of North and South Korea Respectfully. Following a Soviet-backet invasion from North into South Korea the Korean War broke out. The Korean peninsula remained divided And after 3 years of fighting throughout the nation, it was left effectively destroyed, especially in the cities. The conflict persists to this very day, even after the korean and the signing of the Armistice agreement, the 2 governments are still officially at war. As far off as 2018, the leaders of North and South Korea have signed the Panmunjom Declaration stating that they will work towards finding an end to their countries conflict.
