National Animal: Siberian Tiger

The National animal of South Korea, the Siberian Tiger is an important symbol of thier culture. Unfortunately, while there is are no longer any tigers in the wild in South Korea the country was once known as the "Land of Tigers". Previously, Korean people were also knonw as "the people who know how to tame the tiger". A large compliment for Tigers are the largest cats in the world! They have a striking pattern of orange and black stripes that act as camouflage when they hunt alone at night. The Tigers population has seen itself stabilize over the last decade, although the species is still considered endangered.

National Flower: Mugungwha

The Mugungwha also known as the rose of sharon is the national flower of South Korea. The flower in considered an object which represents deep affection. The flower is a strong symbol for love with a meaning which translates to "eternal blossom that never fades". The flower has remained an important symbols in Korean culture for many centuries. Those who cherish the national flower do so because it honors the country's many successes and tribulations and stands as the country's noble spirit throughout its many experiences.

National Sport: Taekwondo

One of Koreas most recognized sports is that of its national sport - Taekwondo. While its origins come from as a form of self-defence it is now an established sport worldwide. The word "Taekwondo" consists of the words for "foot", "fist" and "art". A more direct translation could be "the art of the foot and the fist". A match of Taekwondo has 2 participants. The goal is to use kicking and punching on the opponents head and torso to gain points while avoiding from being struck yourself. Matches have three 2 minutes rounds with one-minute breaks in between the rounds. Extra points are awarded to players who perform more advance techniques to land hits while faliing or exiting the matted area results in penalisation.

National Food: Kimchi

While alos the name of the national dish of South Korea, Kimchi also refers to the process of fermenting vegetables. Kimchi is typically made with napa cabbage, Korean radish and sometimes cucumberand is traditionally made by adding a paste of chili garlic and fish sauce to this mix. The fermenting process has a wide range of timespans including several months all the way up to several years. Korean ancestors once practiced burying kimchi jars in the ground during the winter season, as fermented foods can keep for several years. The benfits of eating Kimchi come from that it is a low calorie, low fat and cholesterol free food. It is very healthy and a widely enjoyed food throughout the country though it has seen little promotion to countries overseas.

Korean Dishes

Bibimbap

The term "bibim" meaning "mixing" and "bap" meaning cooked rice. Bibimbap is a bowl of warm rice topped with sauted and seasoned vegetables. Common additions include raw / fried egg and sliced beef.

Key Ingredients
  • Vegetables: such as Beansprouts, Spinach, Cucumber, Carrots
  • Beef
  • White Rice
Sundae

Commonly, a popular street food. Sundae is a form of blood sausage, typically made from cow or pig intestines stuffed with various ingredients

Key Ingredients
  • Intestines, seonji (blood) and minced meats
  • White rice / Cellophane noodles
  • Stuffings: Soybean paste, Barley, Soybean Sprouts, etc
